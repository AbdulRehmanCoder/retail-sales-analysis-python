# Retail Sales Analysis Using Python
## Project Overview
This project analyzes overall sales performance to identify key revenue drivers and time-based trends. By evaluating product-level data and seasonal patterns, it uncovers actionable insights to optimize business strategy. The goal is to pinpoint growth opportunities and provide a data-backed foundation for operational improvement.
## Business Objective
The objective of this analysis is to identify top-performing products, analyze sales trends over time, and uncover insights that can help improve business performance.
## Tools
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
## Analysis Performed
- Data Cleaning And Preprocessing
- Revenue Analysis
- Product Category Analysis
- Monthly Sales Trend Analysis
- Visualization Of Key Metrics
## Key Insights
- Revenue Trends: Total revenue hit 10.03M, with a peak 47.09% contribution in 2004 followed by a sharp drop to 17.86% in 2005.  
- Product Dominance: The "Classic Cars" line is the primary driver, alone generating 39.07% of total revenue and 35% of unit sales.  
- Growth & Seasonality: Analysis shows a mild upward growth trend over time with recurring seasonal spikes suggesting predictable annual patterns.  
- Dependency Risk: Three out of seven product lines account for 65% of total volume, indicating a high reliance on a small group of products.
## Conclusion
The analysis highlights the products and categories that drive most revenue and identifies patterns in sales performance. These insights can help business focus on high-performing products and improve their sales strategy.
